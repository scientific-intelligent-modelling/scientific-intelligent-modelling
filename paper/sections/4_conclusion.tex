\section{Conclusion}

We presented SR-Workbench, a unified symbolic regression research platform that standardizes heterogeneous methods, datasets, and recurring research operations under a common substrate. Rather than introducing another symbolic regression model, we focus on the infrastructure bottleneck that slows down SR research: repository fragmentation, environment incompatibility, inconsistent dataset conventions, and weak protocol control.

The current version already demonstrates three practical capabilities: integration of diverse SR methods behind a common execution interface, schema-level dataset validation including formula-grounded verification, and pilot end-to-end runs that expose both ID and OOD behavior under a shared result format.

At the same time, we deliberately treat the current paper as a first systems release rather than a final benchmarking claim. The next iteration should strengthen frozen-budget comparisons across all supported methods, expression normalization and complexity analysis, protocol compilation, and reviewer-guided improvement scheduling.

The broader message is simple: in symbolic regression, progress is often limited not only by search algorithms, but also by the absence of reliable research infrastructure. By making method onboarding, dataset canonicalization, validation, and iterative execution explicit and reusable, SR-Workbench provides a concrete step toward faster, more reproducible, and more extensible SR research.
